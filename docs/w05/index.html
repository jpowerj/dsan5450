ProPublica vs. Northpointe

  • Example with 1000s of books, papers, discussions around it!
    • üö©: obsession with one example may conceal wider range of issues
  • Northpointe created a ML algorithm called COMPAS, used by court systems all over the US to predict ‚Äúrisk‚Äù of arrestees
  • In 2016, ProPublica published investigative report documenting COMPAS‚Äôs racial discrimination, in violating equal error rates between black and white arrestees
  • Northpointe responded that COMPAS does not discriminate, as it satisfies calibration
  • People have argued about who is ‚Äúright‚Äù for 8 years, with some progress, but‚Ä¶ not a lot

COMPAS Overview

When defendants are booked into jail in Broward County, Florida, they are asked to respond to a COMPAS questionnaire with 137 questions, including ‚ÄúWas one of your parents ever sent to jail or prison?,‚Äù ‚ÄúHow many of your friends/acquaintances are taking drugs illegally?,‚Äù and ‚ÄúHow often did you get into fights at school?‚Äù Arrestees are also asked to agree or disagree with the statements ‚ÄúA hungry person has the right to steal‚Äù and ‚ÄúIf people make me angry or I lose my temper, I can be dangerous.‚Äù

Answers are fed into the COMPAS model, which generates an individual risk score reported in three buckets: ‚Äúlow risk‚Äù (1 to 4), ‚Äúmedium risk‚Äù (5 to 7), and ‚Äúhigh risk‚Äù (8 to 10).

COMPAS Overview

ProPublica accused COMPAS of racism: ‚ÄúThere‚Äôs software used across the country to predict future criminals. And it‚Äôs biased against blacks,‚Äù read the subheading on the article.

ProPublica found that COMPAS‚Äôs error rates‚Äîthe rate at which the model got it wrong‚Äîwere unequal across racial groups. COMPAS‚Äôs predictions were more likely to incorrectly label African Americans as high risk and more likely to incorrectly label white Americans as low risk.

‚ÄúIn the criminal justice context,‚Äù said Julia Angwin, coauthor of the ProPublica article, ‚Äúfalse findings can have far-reaching effects on the lives of the people charged with crimes.‚Äù
